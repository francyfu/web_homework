The S&P 500, for example, rose each day last week, even recovering Friday from a midday drop following a Bloomberg report that President Donald Trump still wants to impose tariffs on an additional $200 billion worth of imports from China. Earlier in the week, it appeared both countries had softened their stances enough to give investors hope that a deal still could be struck. That might still be the case. Though Friday’s comment threw cold water on trade optimism, this could just be more of the president’s arm-twisting negotiating style before an agreement is reached, says Peter Jankovskis, co-chief investment officer at OakBrook Investments. For the week, the Dow Jones Industrial Average rose about 1%, or 238 points, to 26,154, its highest close since Feb. 1. The S&P 500 also gained about 1%, or 33, to 2904.98, its second-highest close. The Nasdaq Composite tacked on 107.50, to 8010.04. The fundamentals of the economy and business are stable and “very positive,” notes Margaret Patel, senior portfolio manager at Wells Fargo Asset Management. For example, August economic data out this past week was almost one-way positive: U.S. industrial production rose 0.4%, above the consensus of 0.3%; consumer prices rose 0.2%, below expectations of 0.3%; and the producer-price index also came in lower than anticipated.
